## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are**: Small groups traveling together — friends, couples, families, or co-workers on a trip. Solo travelers are supported but the product is designed around the group experience. Users are not finance-minded; they are travelers who happen to need to split costs.

**Context of use**:
- **Primary**: On-the-go, during the trip. A user stands outside a 便利商店 / 居酒屋 / 車站 and logs an expense they just paid for. Phone-in-hand, one-handed use, possibly outdoors, possibly tired, possibly tipsy.
- **Secondary**: Settlement viewing happens anywhere, anytime — often on the last night of the trip or after returning home. It is a moment of "let's wrap up" rather than active input.

**Job to be done**: "Record this expense in 10 seconds so I can get back to enjoying the trip," and later, "Show me who owes whom, without making me think about accounting."

### Brand Personality

**3 words (concrete, not abstract)**:
1. **清新舒服** — breathable, uncluttered, calm
2. **日系無印良品風** — understated, honest, quiet confidence, paper-and-pencil restraint
3. **清爽夏天感** — summer-afternoon light, sea-breeze ease, nothing sticky or heavy

**Physical-object analogy**: A travel journal you'd find in a 文房具店 on a July afternoon — cream paper, one or two soft ink colors, a thin ribbon bookmark. Not a luxury leather notebook. Not a slick tech gadget. Something you'd actually throw in a tote bag.

**Emotional goals**: Confidence without pressure. Lightness. A little nostalgic without being retro. The feeling of being on vacation *while using the app*, not the feeling of doing chores.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone**: 日系 travel journal × early summer. Kiki's Delivery Service (魔女宅急便) palette as the north star — sea blues, soft sky, fresh green of the hillside, terracotta/red-bean as a rare warm accent, cream paper as the base canvas.

**Reference**: Kiki's Delivery Service — specifically the clear-sky coastal town scenes (Koriko). The palette's confidence, the unfussy composition, the feeling of midday light.

**Anti-references** (explicit bans):
- **Dark mode** — forbidden. Light mode only, always.
- **Space / futuristic / sci-fi aesthetics** — forbidden. No cyberpunk, no neon, no "techy" glow, no holographic gradients, no synthwave grids.
- **Glassmorphism + glow-on-dark** — forbidden by the above, reiterated.
- **AI-slop tells** — no purple-to-blue gradients, no gradient text, no cyan-on-dark, no rounded-icon-above-every-heading templates.

**Theme**: Light mode only. Off-white / cream base (`--background` is currently `40 33% 94%`, keep this direction). Primary sky-water blue, secondary fresh leaf green, sparingly used terracotta or red-bean warm accent for rare emphasis (e.g., settlement "you owe" states, celebratory moments).

**Typography direction**: Noto Sans TC is load-bearing for zh-TW — keep it. For Latin text and display use, pair with a humanist sans or gentle display face that feels like hand-set printed matter, NOT a reflex default (avoid Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Instrument Sans, Space Grotesk, Fraunces, Playfair, etc.). Think "font you'd see on a well-made stationery label," not "font you'd see on a SaaS landing page."

**Spacing**: Generous. Mobile-first but never cramped — this is a travel journal, not a spreadsheet. Varied vertical rhythm. Avoid card-in-card nesting.

**Motion**: Gentle, natural deceleration (ease-out-quart / expo). No bounce, no elastic, no attention-grabbing page-load choreography. Motion should feel like a page turning, not a notification.

### Design Principles

1. **Breathing room beats density.** Travelers have cognitive load already — the UI should never add to it. If a screen feels busy, cut.
2. **One-handed, one-glance.** Every primary action must be reachable with the thumb and legible in sunlight. Mobile-first is not optional.
3. **Summer-light palette, used with restraint.** Follow the 60/30/10 rule. Cream dominates; blue and green anchor; warm accents are rare and earned.
4. **Paper over glass.** When in doubt, reach for paper-and-ink metaphors over tech/glass metaphors. Subtle textures and honest edges beat blur and gloss.
5. **Never techy, never futuristic.** No neon, no grids-in-perspective, no deep space. If it would belong in a sci-fi dashboard, it does not belong here.
